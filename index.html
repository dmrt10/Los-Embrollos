Los Embrollos

[Diego Murat] (b. 1999, Morelia, México) He studied a Bachelor’s degree in Art and Design at the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) from 2018 to 2022, specializing in Visual Communication Strategies. As a visual artist, he focuses on the study of image and reality, culture, fashion, and identity, mainly through digital media such as photography, video, and 3D rendering. He explores how the image has become the sole source of “life” in our world. The need to keep creating images has led to new forms of representation, making these media essential tools in his work. Concepts like hyperreality, image (representation and symbolism), digital imagery, visual culture, and the cultural impact of the image are key areas of focus. He is interested in addressing the now blurred distinction between the tangible and virtual worlds, using digital imagery as a means to study contemporary culture and aesthetics, fostering new reflective processes through his work.

His work primarily centers on digital media, including 3D modeling, rendering, and animation, as well as photographic and video production and editing, digital graphic design, and visual programming software. He has been involved in the management and production of music events, brand and music artist direction and production, editorial design, and motion graphics creation for prominent brands.
